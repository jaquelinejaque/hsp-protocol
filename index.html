The Problem

AI Agents Are Talking to Each Other.
Who's Watching?

As MCP, A2A, and ANP protocols enable autonomous agent-to-agent communication, a critical question remains unanswered: How do humans stay in control?

🔄

Infinite Loops

AI agents negotiating endlessly without human intervention, consuming resources and making unauthorized commitments.

⚡

Flash Events

Rapid cascading actions between agents—like the 2010 Flash Crash—causing trillion-dollar consequences in minutes.

🚫

Bypassable Oversight

Current "human-in-the-loop" systems can be configured, minimized, or disabled. That's not real oversight.

The Solution

Human Supervision Protocol

A revolutionary framework that makes human oversight mandatory at the architectural level—impossible to bypass, disable, or configure away.

1

Mandatory Approval

Human approval required for all actions above baseline risk. No exceptions. No bypass mechanisms. No "emergency override."

2

Fail-Closed Default

Any timeout, error, or absence of explicit approval results in action DENIAL. Never autonomous execution. Ever.

3

3-Cycle Anti-Loop

AI-to-AI negotiations limited to 3 cycles before mandatory human intervention. Prevents runaway agent interactions.

4

4-Tier Risk Classification

LOW, MEDIUM, HIGH, CRITICAL — each with specific approval requirements, timeout periods, and escalation paths.

5

Multi-Sig Quorum

For Level 4 (Critical) risks, HSP enforces a 3-Key Rule. Dynamic requirement for multiple human signatures before execution.
